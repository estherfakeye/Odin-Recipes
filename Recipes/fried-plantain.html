Description

Fried plantain is a beloved side dish known for its sweet taste, golden color, and crisp edges. Whether served with rice, beans, or eggs, it adds a touch of warmth and satisfaction to any meal. Prepared with ripe plantains and a dash of salt, itâ€™s simple yet packed with flavor.

This dish is a staple in many homes and can be enjoyed at breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Perfectly fried and lightly caramelized, these plantain slices bring comfort, color, and a hint of sweetness to your plate.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe plantains (yellow with black spots)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Peel the plantains by cutting off both ends, then make a shallow slit along the skin and remove it gently.
  2. Slice the plantains diagonally or into round pieces, about half an inch thick.
  3. Sprinkle a small amount of salt over the slices and mix gently to coat.
  4. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Fry the plantains in batches, flipping each piece once, until both sides are golden brown.
  6. Remove and drain on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  7. Serve hot as a side dish or enjoy as a snack!
