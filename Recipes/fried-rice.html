Description

Fried Rice is a colorful, flavorful dish often served at parties, celebrations, and Sunday lunch tables. Made with parboiled rice, mixed vegetables, and seasoned proteins, it's a well-loved meal that's both rich and satisfying.

This fried rice recipe blends aroma, color, and taste perfectly. It's easy to prepare and can be served with fried chicken, plantains, or grilled meat — making it a crowd-pleaser for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long grain rice (parboiled)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, sweet corn, green peas)
  • 1/2 cup diced liver or cooked chicken (optional)
  • 1/2 onion (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 seasoning cube
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)

Steps

  1. Rinse and parboil the rice until it's about 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat oil and sauté chopped onions until translucent.
  3. Add curry powder, thyme, and seasoning cube. Stir to combine and release aroma.
  4. Add diced liver or chicken (if using) and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  5. Stir in the mixed vegetables and cook for another 2–3 minutes.
  6. Add the parboiled rice into the mixture, stir well to combine everything evenly.
  7. Cover the pan and allow the rice to steam on low heat for about 10–15 minutes. Stir occasionally.
  8. Once the rice is fully cooked and flavorful, remove from heat and serve warm.
